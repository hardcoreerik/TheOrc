# Licensing

TheOrc is **dual-licensed**. You may use it under the open-source AGPL-3.0 license,
or — if those terms don't fit — under a separate commercial license.

---

## 1. Open source: AGPL-3.0 (default)

Unless you have a signed commercial agreement, your use of TheOrc is governed by the
**GNU Affero General Public License v3.0**. The full text is in [LICENSE](LICENSE).

What AGPL-3.0 means for you in practice:

- **Use it freely.** Run TheOrc on your own machines, for yourself or your team, with no
  cost and no obligation to share anything.
- **Modify it freely.** Change the code however you like for your own use.
- **Share it freely.** Distribute copies, as long as you keep them under AGPL-3.0 and
  include the source.
- **The network clause (the "A" in AGPL).** If you run a **modified** version of TheOrc as
  a service that other people interact with over a network, you must offer those users the
  source code of your modified version. This is the key difference from plain GPL, and it's
  the clause that keeps the project open even when it's used as a hosted service.

If you're an individual, a hobbyist, a researcher, or a team running TheOrc internally on
your own hardware, AGPL-3.0 almost certainly covers everything you want to do at no cost.

---

## 2. Commercial license (optional)

A commercial license is for cases where AGPL-3.0's obligations don't work for you, such as:

- Building a **closed-source** product or service on top of TheOrc.
- Embedding TheOrc into a commercial offering without releasing your own modifications.
- Distributing TheOrc as part of a proprietary application.
- Any situation where the AGPL copyleft or network-source requirement is a blocker.

A commercial license removes the copyleft obligations in exchange for a negotiated
agreement. To inquire, contact **hardcoreerik@gmail.com** with a short description of how
you intend to use TheOrc.

---

## 3. How dual licensing is possible

The project can offer both licenses because all contributors agree to the
[Contributor License Agreement](CLA.md). The CLA grants the project maintainer the rights
needed to license the combined work under both AGPL-3.0 and commercial terms. Without it,
no single party would hold enough rights to offer the commercial option.
